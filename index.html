Jeff Currier was hired as CTO in April 2024 specifically to drive cloud-native transformation. He led the same playbook at Galileo (the SoFi infra subsidiary) and built SoFi Money, a B2C fintech analogue. He came to ship velocity, not maintain status quo. Announcement

04AWS depth, observability maturity
Chime completed full AWS migration in October 2020 (Aurora + serverless + EKS). Open observability roles, on-call rotations referenced in job postings, and an Engineering Services team focused on platform productivity. Resolve plugs in where Chime already runs. AWS case study
05Application engineering as the revenue-pressure team
VP Engineering Can Envarli leads Chime's Member Engagement org, the application-engineering layer where customer-facing reliability translates directly to active member retention. This is exactly where revenue pressure lives. Announcement
06Peer pattern-match: DoorDash
DoorDash, same archetype (B2C transaction-heavy, AWS-native, public, AI-mature), is now a Resolve customer running 87% faster incident investigations. The reference story is in the bank for a Chime conversation. Resolve · DoorDash
VALUE DRIVER HYPOTHESIS

Resolve unlocks the second leg of Chime's AI strategy: production operations.

Chime has proven AI agents work in customer service. The same logic, and the same board-level confidence, should now apply to the people who keep the rails running. Their application teams are absorbing on-call burden into a transaction-critical AWS stack while shipping AI-augmented code at IPO-era velocity. Every escalation is a roadmap distraction; every minute of degradation is revenue exposure and regulatory risk.

Resolve maps to two of the three core value drivers here: developer productivity (application engineers stay on roadmap instead of paged into bridges) and business downtime & risk reduction (every minute of MTTR is dollars and trust). The operational-cost story compounds as the headcount-flat post-IPO reality bites.
